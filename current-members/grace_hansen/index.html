CURRENT MEMBERS

Grace Hansen

Graduate Student

Grace Hansen is an MD/PhD student who joined the lab in 2016. She is interested in identifying gene regulatory networks act in different tissues to regulate human traits. In the Nobrega lab, she has focused on identifying gene regulatory networks that regulate adipocyte differentiation and are involved in fat deposition. In this project, she has identified a subset of Alu elements which regulate female waist-to-hip ratio, and a gene, SNX10, which is required for adipogenesis in females. She is also interested in making biomedical research more equitable, and has been involved in efforts to increase student representation in faculty hiring efforts.

Clinically, Grace is interested in neurodevelopment and psychiatric disorders. She hopes to use her training in genetics to identify gene regulatory networks that regulate human neural development.

Outside of academia, Grace likes to bike, raise moths, train her cat to do tricks, and find little patches of wilderness all around Chicago.
