CURRENT MEMBERS

Ivy Aneas, PhD

Research Scientist

My research interests stem from several GWAS studies that have implicated noncoding single-nucleotide polymorphisms in the risk of common diseases. One of the major hypotheses that we pursue is that variation on these noncoding sequences can have an impact on phenotype by altering expression of nearby genes. In order to identify those potential functional non-coding regions, my projects seek to:

  1. understand how enhancers coordinate to determine a complex pattern of gene expression,
  2. understand the dynamics of gene regulation across time, space and in response to stimuli, and
  3. evaluate the functional impact of regulatory variation in phenotypic variation and human diseases.

While much of my recent experience has included working with cardiovascular biology and genomics of developmental genes, the strategies that I have developed and utilized are readily applicable to a broad spectrum of disciplines.
