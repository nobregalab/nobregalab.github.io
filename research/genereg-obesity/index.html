Genetic Regulation of obesity and fat deposition

A central question in modern human genetics is how to identify the genes that are involved in human disease. This is a challenging topic, as most mutations that cause disease are located in non-coding regions of the genome. These mutations often act by disrupting gene regulators.

Using a combination of statistical genetics and in vitro experiments, our lab identifies and characterizes genes that are involved in obesity, and defines the gene regulatory networks that control weight gain. Using these approaches, we have made some of the major discoveries in the genetics of obesity.
